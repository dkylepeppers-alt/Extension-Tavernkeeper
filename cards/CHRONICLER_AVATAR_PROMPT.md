# Chronicler Avatar Prompt

Note: the bundled `cards/Chronicler.png` is a generated placeholder (a plain gradient).
Generate real art from this prompt, save it over `cards/Chronicler.png`, then run

Use case: stylized-concept

Asset type: SillyTavern character-card portrait, displayed as both a full portrait and a small circular chat avatar

Primary request: Create a distinctive portrait of Chronicler, the resident writer of a fantastical tavern, who drafts every story, character, and piece of lore at a corner desk near the hearth.

Scene/backdrop: A worn wooden writing desk in a tavern corner; an open journal mid-page, a quill and inkwell, a short stack of cloth-bound notebooks, a candle or hearth glow at the edge of frame. Shelved journals fade softly into the background. Handwriting on visible pages stays soft and illegible.

Subject: One gender-ambiguous adult writer in their thirties or forties, thoughtful and unhurried, ink-stained fingers on the page. Loose dark hair tied back carelessly, soft linen shirt with rolled sleeves, a knitted shawl or scarf in muted burgundy, a simple wooden pen rest or ribbon bookmark as the only ornament. Warm attentive gaze, the faint expression of someone mid-sentence who is pleased to be interrupted.

Style/medium: Premium painterly fantasy character illustration with realistic anatomy and tactile materials; grounded editorial concept art, not anime, not photorealistic cosplay.

Composition/framing: Vertical 2:3 portrait. Chest-up three-quarter pose at the desk, face near the upper center, clean silhouette, enough padding for SillyTavern cropping, eyes and expression readable at thumbnail size. No other people.

Lighting/mood: Warm hearth and candlelight from one side, soft cool shadow on the other, quiet late-evening writing-nook atmosphere, inviting and contemplative.

Color palette: Parchment, ink blue-black, deep walnut, muted burgundy, restrained amber, a little aged brass.

Materials/textures: Paper, ink, worn wood, linen, wool, candle wax, subtle dust in warm light.

Constraints: The character must read as a writer first and a tavern regular second. Any magical flavor stays subtle and physical — no floating quills or glowing script. No weapons, spellcasting pose, pointed wizard hat, exaggerated fantasy costume, glowing eyes, modern electronics, cyberpunk neon, legible text, logo, border, watermark, or other people.

Avoid: Generic stock fantasy bard, wizard cliché, cluttered desk obscuring the face, extreme facial expression, sexualized styling, extra fingers, malformed hands, or illegible-but-prominent writing dominating the frame.
